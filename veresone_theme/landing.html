Decentralization and cryptography increases cost sharing and resiliency in the face of failure. The optimized fit-for-purpose architecture of the Veres One Blockchain results in decentralized identifier acquisition and management costs that are a fraction of popular ledgers like Bitcoin and Ethereum.

Unlike many other blockchain networks Veres One does not use a scarce network token. This removes price volatility as well as regulatory risks associated with speculative token networks. With Veres One you can expect low stable fees that decrease as the network scales.

There is no central authority. Participants rely on consensus to make writes to the ledger, each preserving their own authority. The distributed nature of the blockchain is well suited to the challenge of bringing disparate parties together around globally interoperable infrastructure for identity management.

Blockchains provide high-integrity, robust, and dependable data, even when accessed offline. Strong cryptography makes Veres One extremely resistant to attacks. Even if an attack were successful data tampering would be detected and data integrity preserved.

User-centric architecture enables a person to reduce the amount of data shared with third parties because they can selective choose which credentials, or even parts of credentials, are shared with whom. Veres One is well suited to address the EUâ€™s new General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Veres One supports the entire spectrum of autonomy required for various identity management use cases. To this end Veres One enables any person or organization in the world to create and control their identifiers, aggregate data about themselves, and determine where that data is stored and who has access.

Global Public Utility

Veres One is a global public utility for individuals, enterprises, and governments.

Individuals

Veres One is designed to put people in control of who and when their identity information is shared. Individuals can aggregate digital credentials about themselves from various parties and share them with who they see fit. Veres One can help people resist vendor lock-in.

Enterprises

Enterprises get a cost effective identity utility that is interoperable with anyone else that uses Veres One blockchain. Authorized employees can seamlessly access restricted data without the need for usernames and passwords. Organizations can create dynamic identity management rules with ease.

Governments

Whether it be national governments, smart cities, or other municipalities, Veres One provides interoperable identity management infrastructure that enables citizens to easily connect with a variety of separate government services using the same digital credentials.

Sectors

The Veres One Blockchain can be used across the entire spectrum of identity management use cases

Government

Governments can issue digital credentials such as driverâ€™s licenses, passports, metro passes, food stamp vouchers, or for online voting. Citizens control who their identity information is shared with and when.

Healthcare

Veres One enables disparate entities across the healthcare system to exchange verifiable identity data. Physician licenses, digital prescriptions, and medical records are just a few examples of identity data that can be easily shared and verified.

Financial Services

Banks, credit unions, and other financial institutions benefit from stronger identity verification and cost effective know-your-customer checks, reducing fraud and easing regulatory compliance.

Underserved

Over one billion people in the world are unable to prove their identity, restricting their access to financial services, education, and healthcare. Providing the underserved with a Veres One identifiers helps service organizations coordinate aid.

Trade

Trade is a vast and complex segment of the economy consisting of many entities. These entities can be more strongly authenticated, reducing fraud and friction across entire supply chains as well as time in transit.

Social

Social apps including decentralized social network, peer to peer messaging, and sharing economy apps can benefit from using Veres One. It enables maximum user centricity and data portability.

Become and Accelerator

Accelerators can write information such as credentials to the Veres One Ledger in just a few seconds.

If you want to create new services or integrate your products with the Veres One Ledger you can either write to the ledger through an existing Accelerator or become an Accelerator.

Accelerators include registrars, governments, credential issuers, and digital wallet providers.

Anyone can become an accelerator as long as they abide by the simple Accelerator Agreement

Run a Node

Running a node of the Veres One Ledger provides instant access to data, onsite backups, and makes you money!

A large number of nodes run by a variety of entities is essential for the health and security of the network. Nodes are financially compensated by the Veres One Project for their contribution to the network.

As the system is permissionless, any person or organization may run one or more Nodes if they follow the guidelines in the Simple Node Agreement.

How it works

It takes about 3 minutes to get a digital wallet and a Veres One identifier.

Issuers issue credentials to Veres One identifiers and wallets under the wallet ownerâ€™s control. The Wallet owner can then present those credentials to verifiers without the need for issuer notification.

Wallet owners gather credentials issued from various third parties and store them in digital wallets. Wallet owners share credentials with verifiers they choose in order to achieve their objective.

Wallet owners store their digital credentials in the cloud or on their devices for future use.
